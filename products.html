Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our cinnamon products and services.

Ceylon cinnamon (Cinnamomum zeylanicum) is known as "true cinnamon" and has a lighter, more delicate flavor with sweet undertones. It contains lower levels of coumarin, a compound that can be harmful in large doses. Cassia cinnamon (Cinnamomum cassia) has a stronger, more pungent flavor and higher coumarin content. Ceylon is lighter in color and the sticks have thin, paper-like layers, while Cassia is darker and has a thicker, harder bark.

For optimal freshness, store cinnamon in an airtight container away from direct sunlight, heat, and moisture. Keep it in a cool, dark place like a pantry or cupboard. Properly stored, ground cinnamon will maintain its quality for about six months, while cinnamon sticks can last up to a year. For even longer storage, you can refrigerate or freeze cinnamon in airtight containers.
